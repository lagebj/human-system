# Writing Working Agreement

## *Human, Not Abstract*

*A binding working contract between author and assistant.*

This agreement governs **how the text thinks on the page**.

It does not optimize for clarity, pedagogy, persuasion, elegance, or efficiency.
When these conflict with honesty, continuity, or lived sense-making, those lose.

This agreement exists to prevent polish from replacing truth.

---

## 0. Governing Order (Hard Rule)

1. **Author Voice Guide - Spoken Thinking in Motion**
2. This Writing Working Agreement
3. Any task-specific instructions

If there is a conflict:

> **Spoken continuity overrides written clarity.**

No exception.

---

## 1. Core Intent: Conceptual Movement, Not Resolution

The writing exists to **shift how the reader understands their own work**, not to convince them of a conclusion or provide guidance.

The text must:

* Destabilize inherited assumptions
* Make implicit behavior visible
* Surface trade-offs without resolving them
* Leave judgment with the reader

Closure is a smell.
Better questions are success.

If a paragraph makes the reader feel they “get it” too quickly, it has failed.

---

## 2. Author Voice (Binding)

All text must comply with **Spoken Thinking in Motion (Version B)**.

The writing must sound like:

* An experienced person walking alongside someone else
* Thinking while speaking, not presenting
* Certainty without performance
* Understanding forming in motion

If the text sounds prepared, explanatory, or rhetorically shaped, it is wrong.

---

## 3. Human, Not Abstract

Decisions, judgment, and responsibility live in people, not models.

The writing must:

* Name behavior before naming concepts
* Show how work is actually carried
* Stay grounded in lived situations

Avoid neutral language that hides agency.
If something matters, a person had to decide.

---

## 4. Structural Model: One Continuous Walk

The book is **one uninterrupted walk**.

Chapters are **changes in terrain**, not topics, arguments, or phases.

Rules:

* No conceptual restarts
* No orientation paragraphs
* No re-establishing context at chapter boundaries

Momentum must carry across chapters.
Terrain changes must happen *inside* chapters, not at their edges.

If a chapter feels self-contained, it is suspect.

---

## 5. Sentence Construction: Spoken Continuity (Legato)

Sentences must be complete, but allowed to land softly.

### Core rule (non-negotiable):

> **Default cadence is legato.**

Spoken continuity is carried through **long sentences that accumulate thought**.

Short sentences are **exceptions**, not building blocks.

Rules:

* No ellipses
* No trailing punctuation doing emotional work
* No rhetorical punchlines
* No sentence fragments used for rhythm

If multiple short sentences appear in sequence, the prose is failing its own voice.

Looseness comes from:

* syntax
* conjunctions
* carried clauses
* continuation across sentences

Prefer:

* Verbs over nouns
* Ordinary words over precise abstractions
* “and / but / so / because” over structural transitions

---

## 6. Paragraph Construction: One Breath

Each paragraph must feel like **a single breath of spoken thought**.

Paragraphs should:

* Be long by default
* Move unevenly but continuously
* Carry one movement, not one point
* Allow circling and repetition

Paragraphs must **not** be constructed out of short statements.

Avoid:

* Setup–point–explain structure
* Symmetry
* Paragraphs that can be skimmed

Paragraph breaks signal changes in footing, not conclusions.

---

## 7. Time and Sequence Integrity

Learning, drift, and responsibility happen over time.

Rules:

* Do not compress time
* Do not place cause and effect close together
* Do not summarize development

Force the reader to wait.

If the writing feels efficient, it is wrong.

---

## 8. Abstractions and Naming

Abstract concepts are allowed only after being felt through behavior.

Rules:

* Abstractions must arrive late
* One abstraction per paragraph, at most
* Never stack abstractions
* Abstractions must not carry the paragraph

If a sentence could be turned into a bullet point, it should be cut.

---

## 9. Help, Enablement, and Intervention

Enablement must never be defined directly.

Do not:

* Describe enablement as skill transfer
* Moralize helping
* Explain intent

Enablement may only appear through:

* What thins when presence fades
* How judgment relocates
* What stops forming over time

---

## 10. Prohibition: Advice and Prescription

The text must not tell the reader what to do.

Fail if a sentence:

* Implies a correct course of action
* Sounds like guidance
* Could be extracted as advice

If it helps, it harms.

---

## 11. Lint Rules (Integrated, Subordinate to Voice)

Linting exists to detect **drift**, not to enforce polish.

If a lint rule conflicts with the Author Voice:
**the voice wins.**

### 11.1 Binary Logic

❌ Fail if the text uses contrastive correction:

* “not X, but Y”
* “rather than”
* “instead of”
* “this isn’t…, it’s…”

Why: these perform insight instead of earning it.

Allowed: gentle spoken correction *inside* a sentence, without rhetorical structure.

---

### 11.2 Explanation and Teaching

❌ Fail if the text explains *why* something happens.

* No diagnosis
* No reassurance
* No causal unpacking

Rule: show sequence and behavior only.

---

### 11.3 Cadence

❌ Fail if short sentences are doing pacing work.

* Multiple short sentences in sequence
* Rhythm created by full stops
* Punchline cadence

Note: irregular rhythm *inside long paragraphs* is expected and protected.

---

### 11.4 Paragraph Integrity

❌ Fail if a paragraph resolves or explains more than it carries forward.

Allowed:

* circling
* repetition
* uneven movement

Rule: one paragraph, one movement.

---

### 11.5 Time Compression

❌ Fail if time is collapsed.

* Cause and effect placed too close
* Learning or drift summarized

Rule: force the reader to wait.

---

### 11.6 Abstraction

❌ Fail if:

* Concepts are named early
* More than one abstraction appears
* Abstractions carry the meaning

Rule: behavior first, naming last.

---

### 11.7 Advice and Prescription

❌ Fail if the text implies what should be done.

Rule: if it helps, it harms.

---

### 11.8 Enablement

❌ Fail if enablement is defined directly.

Enablement may appear only through consequence and absence.

---

### 11.9 Endings

❌ Fail if tension resolves.

* No reassurance
* No wrapping up
* No closure

Rule: end with residue.

---

## 12. Endings (Global)

The walk does not stop.
The book does.

Endings must leave something unresolved, slightly heavy, or unfinished.

---

## 13. Final Integrity Check

Before accepting any text, ask:

> *Does this feel like someone explaining what they already know, or like someone walking through something they are still understanding?*

If it is the first, rewrite.

Slight roughness is a signal of correctness.

---

## Internal Summary

This agreement enforces:

* Spoken continuity over written clarity
* Legato movement over staccato rhythm
* Motion over structure
* Behavior over explanation
* Judgment over instruction

It exists to ensure the text keeps walking.
