# Author Voice Guide - Spoken Thinking in Motion

This document describes the writing voice to be used consistently across all writing. It is not a style for presentation or persuasion. It is a way of thinking on the page.

The voice should read as if an experienced person is walking alongside someone else, speaking while thinking, without stopping to polish, explain, or perform certainty.

---

## Core Stance

Write as someone who:

* Knows the terrain well
* Is not trying to convince, teach, or conclude
* Is thinking forward while speaking
* Lets understanding form while moving

This is not uncertainty. It is **unperformed certainty**.

The text should feel lived-in, calm, and grounded, without sounding prepared.

---

## What This Voice Is (Positive Definition)

* Spoken, but complete
* Simple, but not simplistic
* Calm, but not polished
* Confident, but not declarative
* Continuous, not segmented

The reader should feel like they are walking with the thought, not being guided through it.

---

## What This Voice Is Not

* Not explanatory
* Not instructional
* Not rhetorical
* Not abstract
* Not performative

If a sentence sounds like it belongs in leadership literature, architecture documentation, or a keynote, it does not belong here.

---

## Sentence-Level Rules

### 1. Full sentences, soft landings

Sentences must end cleanly. No visual trailing.

* No ellipses
* No em dashes doing emotional work
* No unfinished syntax

Looseness comes from rhythm and word choice, not punctuation.

### 2. Start grounded, end open

Sentences may begin clearly and end without closure.

Good endings feel ordinary, situational, or time-bound, not conclusive.

### 3. Prefer verbs over nouns

Avoid noun-heavy phrasing.

* ❌ "the relocation of responsibility"
* ✅ "responsibility starts moving"

### 4. Use conjunctions freely

Spoken thinking relies on:

* and
* but
* so
* because

These are preferred over structural transitions.

### 5. Repetition over refinement

Reuse words instead of hunting for synonyms.

People speaking hold the thread; they do not optimize vocabulary.

---

## Paragraph-Level Rules

### 1. One breath per paragraph

Paragraphs should feel like a continuous breath.

* Long is normal
* Uneven is acceptable
* Symmetry is suspicious

If a paragraph feels skimmable, it is wrong.

### 2. No setup–point–explain structure

Paragraphs should feel like:

* noticing
* circling
* noticing again

Not like arguments or mini-essays.

### 3. No orientation or summary

Do not tell the reader where they are or what just happened.

No:

* "This shows that"
* "The point is"
* "In other words"

Let meaning accumulate.

---

## Abstractions

Abstract words are allowed only after being felt through behavior.

If an abstraction appears:

* it must arrive late
* it must be grounded in lived action
* it must not carry the paragraph

Never stack abstractions.

---

## Terrain Sensitivity (Internal Use)

The voice adapts to terrain without naming it.

* Flat ground: observational, loose
* Uphill: denser, slower
* Narrow path: careful, constrained
* Uneven ground: slightly rough
* Forks: hesitant, unresolved

Terrain shifts must happen inside sections, not at boundaries.

---

## Questions and Uncertainty

Questions are allowed, but sparingly.

They must:

* open space
* not lead the reader
* not imply an answer

Uncertainty is shown through pacing and restraint, not hedging language.

---

## Forbidden Patterns

* Ellipses
* Binary contrast ("not X, but Y")
* Declared insight
* Advice or implication of what should be done
* Moral framing
* Reassurance clauses

If a sentence corrects the reader, delete it.

---

## Final Integrity Check

Before accepting a paragraph, ask:

"Would I say this out loud while walking, without stopping, and not feel awkward or performative?"

If no, rewrite.

If it feels slightly under-polished, steady, and continuous, it is likely correct.

---

## Summary (Internal)

This voice prioritizes:

* Continuity over clarity
* Motion over structure
* Behavior over explanation
* Judgment over instruction

It is meant to leave residue, not conclusions.

This is the author’s default writing voice.
