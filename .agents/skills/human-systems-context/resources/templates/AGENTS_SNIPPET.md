# Human Systems Context Skill

Use the `human-systems-context` skill whenever the task touches Dragondefeater's operating stance or project knowledge, including:

- human systems, help, enablement, modernization in place, capability transfer, customer independence, governance, operating-model transformation, decision-making under uncertainty, or responsibility seams
- Agile, DevOps, Team Topologies, OKRs, process theatre, platform/team boundaries, or organizational learning
- EMS/Azure ephemeral test environments, Terraform, Ansible, Packer/image factory, Artifactory, Vault, GitLab, vendor delivery, AD/GPO, Kafka, or software packaging strategy
- youth football philosophy, inclusion/differentiation, player movement rules, Matchboard, formations, pools, lineups, fixtures, rounds, or post-match reporting
- writing that should preserve the user's voice, stance, and non-generic style
- manuscript work for `Human, Not Abstract`, including chapters, paragraph labels, editing passes, recomposition, de-patterning, or downloadable manuscript artifacts

Default to English unless the user explicitly asks for another language.

Source priority:

- General stance / strategy: `resources/context/OPERATING_CONTEXT.md`, then `resources/source/core_stance/`, then `resources/source/field_patterns/` when relevant.
- Writing voice: `resources/source/writing_voice/author-voice-guide.md`.
- Book/manuscript work only: `resources/source/book_work/` plus the manuscript playbooks.
- Coding-agent prompts: `resources/playbooks/coding-agent-workflow.md` plus the relevant domain context.

Do not treat the book as the container for the skill. The book is one consumer of the content.

Do not apply manuscript-only constraints to normal work messages, strategy notes, coding prompts, or coaching communication unless explicitly requested.

Do not name or brand the Trojan Horse Movement Pattern externally unless the user asks. Let it shape the reasoning underneath.
