# Pattern Ledger

**Binding document.** Once a pattern is recorded here, it is illegal everywhere in the manuscript.

This ledger exists to prevent prefab shapes from re-entering the text after they have been identified and removed.

---

## How to Use

When a pattern is identified during an editing pass:

1. Name the pattern
2. Note where it appeared
3. Explain why it is a pattern (what it flattens, what it avoids)
4. Record the date and pass
5. Add to this ledger

Once added, the pattern must not reappear. If it does, it is a violation that must be corrected immediately.

## Recorded Patterns

### Binary Contrast Pattern ("not X, but Y")

**First identified:** 2026-06-12, Initial Draft, Paragraph 1

**Pattern:** Using contrastive correction to perform insight
- "not what is broken but what has formed"
- "not X, but Y"
- "rather than"
- "instead of"
- "this isn't…, it's…"

**Why it is a pattern:** These structures perform insight instead of earning it. They create a rhetorical move that feels like clarity but is actually a shortcut. The reader is told what to think rather than being walked through the observation.

**How to avoid:** State what is observed directly. Let the contrast exist in the behavior, not in the sentence structure.

**Status:** **BANNED** - This pattern is illegal everywhere in the manuscript.

## Pattern Categories

Patterns typically fall into these categories:

### Rhetorical Structures
- Binary contrasts ("not X, but Y")
- Setup-point-explain paragraph arcs
- Punchline endings
- Tidy resolutions

### Sentence Mechanics
- Repeated opening phrases
- Symmetrical sentence lengths
- Rhythm created by full stops
- Fragments used for effect

### Conceptual Moves
- Defining enablement directly
- Turning insight into advice
- Explaining why instead of showing what
- Compressing time between cause and effect

### Paragraph Shapes
- Uniform paragraph lengths
- Visual balance over thought breaths
- Short paragraphs doing pacing work
- Self-contained paragraph arguments
